<p><p>The human rabies vaccine market includes both pre-exposure and post-exposure vaccines. Pre-exposure vaccines are administered to individuals at high risk of contracting rabies, such as laboratory workers and veterinarians, to provide immunity before exposure to the virus. Post-exposure vaccines are given to individuals after potential exposure to rabies, such as through animal bites, to prevent the development of the disease. Both types of vaccines are crucial in preventing the spread of rabies in human populations.</p></p>

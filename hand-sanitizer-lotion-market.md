<p><p>Hand sanitizer lotions can be broadly categorized into two types: alcohol-based and alcohol-free. Alcohol-based hand sanitizers typically contain ethanol or isopropyl alcohol as the active ingredient, which effectively kills germs and bacteria on the skin. On the other hand, alcohol-free hand sanitizers utilize alternative ingredients such as benzalkonium chloride or triclosan to achieve similar germ-killing effects without the use of alcohol. Both types of hand sanitizer lotions are popular choices for consumers seeking to maintain hand hygiene and prevent the spread of infections.</p></p>

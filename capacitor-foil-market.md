<p><p>Capacitor foils are typically divided into Cathode Foil and Anode Foil markets. Cathode Foil is used as the negative electrode in capacitors, while Anode Foil serves as the positive electrode. Cathode Foil is commonly made from high-purity aluminum and is thin in design to maximize capacitance. Anode Foil, on the other hand, is usually made from materials like tantalum or niobium and has a thicker construction to withstand high voltages. Both types of foils play crucial roles in the performance and efficiency of capacitors.</p></p>

<p><p>Chicken vaccines are categorized into two main types: live vaccines and killed vaccines. Live vaccines contain weakened forms of the virus, which stimulate an immune response in the chicken without causing disease. On the other hand, killed vaccines contain inactivated virus particles, which also trigger an immune response in the chicken. Both types of vaccines are important tools in preventing diseases in poultry, with live vaccines often providing more long-lasting immunity and killed vaccines being safer for use in young or immunocompromised birds.</p></p>

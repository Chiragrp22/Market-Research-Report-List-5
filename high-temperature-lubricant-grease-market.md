<p><p>High temperature lubricants and greases are classified based on the type of thickener used in their formulation. Soap thickener greases are made using metal soaps, such as lithium or calcium, which provide excellent resistance to water and oxidation. Non-soap thickener greases, on the other hand, use inorganic or organic compounds as thickeners, offering superior high temperature performance and compatibility with a wider range of chemicals. Each type of thickener has unique properties that make them suitable for specific applications in industries such as automotive, aerospace, and manufacturing.</p></p>

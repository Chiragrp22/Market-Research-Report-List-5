<p><p>Digital Polymerase Chain Reaction (dPCR) is a technique used to quantify and amplify DNA samples by partitioning them into numerous individual PCR reactions. There are two main types of dPCR markets: Droplet Digital PCR, which relies on oil droplets for sample partitioning, and Chip-based Digital PCR, which uses microfluidic chips for partitioning. Droplet Digital PCR offers higher sensitivity and precision due to the isolated droplets, while Chip-based Digital PCR allows for high-throughput analysis and miniaturization of the process.</p></p>

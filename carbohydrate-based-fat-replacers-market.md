<p><p>Carbohydrate-based fat replacers are categorized into different types based on their composition and mechanism of action. Starch fat replacers are made from modified starches that mimic the texture and mouthfeel of fats. Fibrous fat replacers are derived from plant fibers and provide a similar bulkiness to fats. Colloidal fat replacers are emulsions or gels made from proteins and carbohydrates. There are also other types of fat replacers available in the market that use various ingredients such as gums, pectin, and cellulose to mimic the texture and functionality of fats in food products.</p></p>

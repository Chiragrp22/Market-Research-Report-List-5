<p><p>Rubber shock absorbers are used to absorb and dampen the impact of shocks and vibrations in various applications. The market is segmented into mechanical, hydraulic, pneumatic, and other types of shock absorbers. Mechanical shock absorbers rely on springs or rubber mounts to absorb energy. Hydraulic shock absorbers use fluid to dampen and dissipate energy. Pneumatic shock absorbers use air to cushion shocks. Other types of shock absorbers include electromagnetic and passive hydraulic systems. Each type offers unique advantages and is suited for specific applications.</p></p>

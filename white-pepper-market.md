<p><p>Whole white pepper refers to the dried berries of the pepper plant, with the outer black husk removed to reveal a creamy white inner core. It is commonly used in dishes where a milder pepper flavor is desired. Ground white pepper, on the other hand, is made by grinding the whole white peppercorns into a fine powder. It is often preferred for its ability to blend seamlessly into dishes without adding black specks like black pepper does. Both whole and ground white pepper are popular in various cuisines worldwide.</p></p>

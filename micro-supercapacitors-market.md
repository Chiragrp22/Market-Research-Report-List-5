<p><p>Micro supercapacitors are compact energy storage devices that come in various types based on their electrode composition and structure. The first type consists of thin film electrodes made of nanostructured materials, allowing for high surface area and capacitance. The second type features in-plane interdigital electrodes, enabling efficient charge transfer. The third type incorporates three-dimensional (3D) electrodes, increasing energy density and power performance. These different types cater to various applications requiring small size and high energy storage capabilities.</p></p>

<p><p>X-ray contrast agents are substances used to improve the visibility of internal body structures during medical imaging procedures. Two common types are medical barium sulfate and iodine-based contrast agents. Barium sulfate is often used for imaging the gastrointestinal tract due to its radiopacity, while iodine-based contrast agents are used for imaging blood vessels and organs. Both types of contrast agents enhance the contrast between the target structure and surrounding tissues, aiding in accurate diagnostics and treatment planning in various medical conditions.</p></p>
